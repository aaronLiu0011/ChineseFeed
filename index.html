Market Concentration

Market concentration in China's feed industry is gradually increasing. In 2024, feed production plants with an annual output exceeding 100,000 tons accounted for 61.8% of the national total production.[3] There are 34 feed enterprise groups with an annual output of over one million tons, of which 7 have an annual output exceeding 10 million tons.[3] This reflects the strengthening of industry integration and economies of scale.

Supply & Demand Dynamics Analysis

This section explores the core driving factors of supply and demand in China's feed market. The demand side primarily analyzes the scale and cyclical fluctuations of downstream pig and poultry farming and their impact on feed demand. The supply side focuses on feed production capacity, regional distribution, and the supply and price changes of key raw materials (such as corn and soybean meal).

Demand Side: Downstream Animal Husbandry

Pig Farming: China is the world's largest pig farming country. The "pig cycle" is significant, and pig price fluctuations directly affect feed demand. The industry is moving towards "integrated farming and slaughtering" to mitigate risks.[4]

Sow Stock: The stock of breeding sows is a core component ensuring pig production capacity. Its fluctuations directly impact future pig slaughter volume and feed demand.

Poultry Farming: The scale of poultry farming is enormous, with poultry meat and egg production leading the world.[5] The industry is accelerating its transformation towards standardization, large-scale, intelligent, and green farming.[6]

In-production Layer Stock: The number of in-production laying hens directly affects egg production and layer feed demand.

Supply Side: Production & Raw Materials

Production Capacity and Regional Distribution: China has immense feed production capacity.[2] Provinces like Shandong and Guangdong lead in output, forming regional production clusters.[3]

Raw Material Supply and Prices: There is a high dependence on imported protein feeds (e.g., soybeans).[2] Prices of raw materials like corn and soybean meal fluctuate significantly, affecting feed costs.[7] Companies are responding by using financial tools like futures hedging and promoting soybean meal reduction and substitution.[2, 8]

Channel Evolution Analysis

This section delves into the evolution of distribution channels in China's feed market. From traditional direct sales and dealer networks to thriving e-commerce channels and various emerging innovative models. It focuses on analyzing the diversified operational models of e-commerce channels, the advantages and disadvantages of online sales, and farmers' acceptance of online purchasing behavior.

Traditional Channel Transformation

Traditional dealer models face challenges [9], leading companies are increasing their direct sales ratio (e.g., Dabeinong's pig feed direct sales exceeding 40% [9]), and shifting from "selling products" to "providing comprehensive services" (such as pig farm management, financing, etc.) [9] to enhance customer loyalty. Large farming groups have strong bargaining power [10], while aquatic farming is still dominated by small and medium-sized farmers, giving feed companies more pricing power and higher profit margins in aquatic feed sales.[10, 11]
